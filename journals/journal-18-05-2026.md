My idea to prevent horrible feedback is to just include a noise gate, where the source signal controls the gate on the distorted signal. Ideally this should clamp the output shut when the input is low and thus no feedback.

I found a schematic online, and did some work to convert it such that my input can be different than the gate input:
![Gate circuit](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019e3a3a-1fd6-709b-a3c2-c1eb84e248a6/paste-1779093347255.png)

The pink signal is modulated by the blue one, where blue drops out, so does pink. The light blue is the gate, once this reaches a threshold of a bit over 0v, it cuts out the main input.

I have massively shrunk the capacitors for this demonstration, so it comes on and off quite agressively but the point stands.

And after a bit of trial and error, I've come up with this:
![Noise gate in contex](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019e3a45-ca64-7fc4-bae0-f903bad07c65/paste-1779094111916.png)

It 100% sounds better than before, so thats amazing, very happy with this addition. In order to get it to have a good degree of reduction, it does eat up quite a bit of current however. I've currently dialed it to ~20mA but I might reduce this in the future.
