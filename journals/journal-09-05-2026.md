# DIST Journal 1 - 09/05/2026

Well! It's time for the not-so-long awaited sequel to VCO: DIST.

This is going to be a distortion eurorack module, not entirely sure what / how its gonna work but that's what today is for!
Overall very excited for this project, I've done a bit of distortion stuff before but this will be on a whole other level. I think for this one, my process will also be slightly different; i'll likely do a bit less sim work and more prototype sections on the breadboard, as seeing how things sound is quite important for distortions.

Before I move onto the design requirements, I'd like to take a little bit of a look at other eurorack distortion modules, just to get a wee bit of an idea of how they do it.

Welp, I've had a brief look at some modules and I think I'm ready to do some requirements :)

## Design Requirements

- Versatile (In the usable sense) - Wacky distortions are cool and all, but first and foremost I need something that can do all the basic sounds, pretty much just can do overdrive and medium distortion.
- Multi stage - I really like the idea of how the Noise Engineering Terci Ruina does it, where you can input at the start of any stage and output at the end of any stage.
- Tone adjustable - Really just need some filtering / eq on board, I really enjoy pedals which have this

---
Those are pretty much all my super basic requirements, the following are optional but I'd quite like to have them.

- Can do heavy distortion - I quite like distortions with insane levels of breakup, so that could be quite fun to include.
- Visual feedback - Could be fun to have like an led or something, might not be worth the effort though lol
- Fun feature - Just something that sounds funky idk what yet, maybe a phasy thing?
